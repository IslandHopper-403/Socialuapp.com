Getting Around Hoi An: Complete Transport Guide (Bikes, Scooters & More)

Everything you need to know about transportation in Hoi An - rental prices, safety tips, and the best routes to explore.

By SocialU Travel Team Updated Sep 20, 2025

One of the best things about Hoi An is how easy it is to get around. The Ancient Town is pedestrian-friendly, the surrounding countryside is flat and perfect for cycling, and renting a scooter opens up the entire region for exploration. This guide covers everything you need to know about transportation in Hoi An - from walking the Ancient Town to riding a scooter to the beach.

Hoi An is compact and easy to navigate. Here's a quick rundown of your options:

Walking (Free)

Best for: Ancient Town exploration, short distances
Range: 1-2 km comfortably
Pros: Free, intimate experience, no parking hassles
Cons: Limited range, can be hot during midday

Scooter/Motorbike (100,000-150,000 VND/day = $4-6)

Best for: Freedom to explore widely, day trips, beach hopping
Range: 30-50 km easily

Taxi/Grab (50,000-150,000 VND per trip = $2-6)

Best for: Airport transfers, short trips with luggage, late nights
Range: Anywhere
Pros: Comfortable, no driving stress, good for groups
Cons: Adds up quickly, less adventurous

üö∂ Walking in Hoi An

Ancient Town Walking

The Ancient Town is wonderfully pedestrian-friendly. In fact, many streets prohibit motorbikes during peak hours (9 AM - 11 AM and 3 PM - 9:30 PM), making walking the best option.

How long to walk across Ancient Town: 15-20 minutes end to end

Best Walking Routes:

1. Classic Ancient Town Loop (2 km, 1 hour)

Start at Japanese Bridge ‚Üí Tran Phu Street ‚Üí Thu Bon riverside ‚Üí An Hoi Bridge ‚Üí back through side streets. Perfect for first-time visitors.

2. Riverside Walk (3 km, 1.5 hours)

Follow the Thu Bon River from Japanese Bridge south to Cam Nam Island and back. Beautiful at sunset with fewer crowds.

3. French Quarter Stroll (1.5 km, 45 min)

Explore the quieter French Quarter with colonial architecture and fewer tourists. Start from Tran Hung Dao Street.

Walking Tips:

  • Walk early morning (6-8 AM) or evening (after 4 PM) to avoid midday heat
  • Wear comfortable shoes - Ancient Town streets are uneven
  • Stay hydrated - buy water at local shops (10,000 VND)
  • Watch for motorbikes even on "pedestrian" streets
  • Use Google Maps offline (download Hoi An map before you go)

üö≤ Renting & Riding Bicycles

Where to Rent Bicycles

1. Your Accommodation (Easiest)
Most hotels, hostels, and guesthouses offer free or cheap bike rentals (0-30,000 VND/day). Quality varies - check brakes and tires before riding.

2. Dedicated Rental Shops
Better quality bikes with gears and better condition. Usually 50,000-80,000 VND/day. Found throughout the Ancient Town and An Hoi Island.

3. Street Vendors
Look for "Bike for Rent" signs. Negotiate prices - start at 30,000 VND/day. Basic bikes but functional.

What to Check Before Renting:

  • ‚úÖ Brakes work properly (test both front and rear)
  • ‚úÖ Tires properly inflated
  • ‚úÖ Seat adjusts to comfortable height
  • ‚úÖ Chain is oiled and functions smoothly
  • ‚úÖ Bell works (important in Vietnam!)
  • ‚úÖ Basket or rack if you need to carry things
  • ‚úÖ Lock included

Best Cycling Routes

1. Rice Paddy Loop (8 km, 1 hour)

The classic Hoi An countryside experience. Head north from Ancient Town through Cam Thanh village, weaving through emerald rice paddies and water buffalo. Flat and easy.

Route: Ancient Town ‚Üí Nguyen Phuc Chu Street ‚Üí Cam Thanh ‚Üí loop back via Hai Ba Trung
Best time: Early morning (6-8 AM) or late afternoon (4-6 PM)
Difficulty: Easy

2. An Bang Beach Ride (5 km one way, 40 min)

Flat, straight road to the beach. Perfect for a morning ride followed by beach time and lunch.

Route: Ancient Town ‚Üí Cua Dai Road ‚Üí An Bang Beach
Best time: Morning (7-9 AM) before it gets hot
Difficulty: Easy
Tip: Lock your bike at a beach restaurant (they'll watch it while you swim)

3. Tra Que Vegetable Village (3 km, 30 min)

Visit the famous organic vegetable gardens. Many farms offer tours and cooking classes.

Route: Ancient Town ‚Üí Tra Que Road (follow signs)
Best time: Morning (7-10 AM) when farmers are working
Difficulty: Easy
Cost: Some farms charge 20,000-50,000 VND entry

Cycling Safety Tips:

  • Always use your bell - locals expect it
  • Ride on the right side of the road
  • Watch for sudden turns from motorbikes
  • Avoid cycling after dark (poor street lighting)
  • Lock your bike whenever you leave it
  • Carry water - it gets hot!
  • Use sunscreen - you'll burn even while moving

üõµ Scooter Rental Guide

The Legal Situation

Important: Technically, you need a Vietnamese driving license or an International Driving Permit (IDP) to legally ride a scooter in Vietnam. In practice, many tourists ride without one, but:

  • Police sometimes set up checkpoints and fine tourists 500,000-2,000,000 VND ($20-80)
  • Your travel insurance likely won't cover you without proper license
  • If you have an accident without a license, you're liable for all costs

Our advice: Get an IDP before traveling ($20-30 in your home country) or stick to bicycles. If you do ride without one, be extra cautious and consider the risks.

Where to Rent Scooters

Rental Shop Prices:

  • Automatic scooter: 100,000-150,000 VND/day ($4-6)
  • Semi-automatic: 80,000-120,000 VND/day ($3-5)
  • Week rental: 600,000-800,000 VND ($24-32)
  • Month rental: 1,500,000-2,000,000 VND ($60-80)

What's Usually Included:

  • Helmet (legally required - use it!)
  • Lock
  • Basic insurance (check what's covered)

What You'll Need:

  • Passport (some shops keep a photocopy, others require the original as deposit)
  • Cash deposit: 1,000,000-3,000,000 VND ($40-120) OR passport
  • Payment upfront or daily

Before You Ride - Inspection Checklist:

  • ‚úÖ Test brakes thoroughly (most important!)
  • ‚úÖ Check fuel level
  • ‚úÖ Test horn
  • ‚úÖ Check all lights work
  • ‚úÖ Inspect tires for wear and pressure
  • ‚úÖ Take photos of existing damage
  • ‚úÖ Ensure you know how to start/stop
  • ‚úÖ Adjust mirrors
  • ‚úÖ Test kickstand

Fuel Costs

Gas is cheap in Vietnam:

  • Current price: ~24,000 VND/liter ($1)
  • Average scooter: 2.5-3 liters per 100 km
  • Daily riding cost: 20,000-50,000 VND ($1-2)

Where to fuel up: Look for green "XƒÉng" (gas) signs. Gas stations are everywhere. Full tank costs ~60,000-80,000 VND ($2.50-3.50).

Best Scooter Day Trips from Hoi An

1. Marble Mountains (15 km, 25 min)

Stunning cave pagodas with panoramic views. Entry 40,000 VND.

2. My Son Sanctuary (40 km, 1 hour)

Ancient Hindu temples - UNESCO World Heritage site. Entry 150,000 VND. Best to go early (7-8 AM) before tour buses arrive.

3. Ba Na Hills (80 km, 1.5 hours)

Famous for the Golden Bridge. Long ride but doable. Cable car ticket 750,000 VND.

4. Beach Hopping

Hit multiple beaches in one day: An Bang ‚Üí Cua Dai ‚Üí Hidden Beach ‚Üí An Bang for sunset. Bring sunscreen and cash for beach chairs/food.

Scooter Safety (READ THIS!)

  • Always wear helmet - not just for fines, for your safety
  • Drive defensively - assume no one sees you
  • Avoid night riding - poor lighting and drunk drivers
  • Don't speed - roads have unexpected hazards
  • Watch for gravel - especially on turns
  • Horn liberally - it's expected, not rude
  • Give way to bigger vehicles - might is right in Vietnam
  • Don't ride in rain - roads become extremely slippery
  • Keep documents on you - license, passport copy, insurance
  • Park in designated areas - street parking fees 5,000-10,000 VND

üöï Taxis & Grab (Ride-Hailing)

Grab (Best Option)

Grab is Southeast Asia's Uber. Download the app before you arrive.

Typical Grab Prices:

  • Within Ancient Town: Not possible (pedestrian zone)
  • Accommodation to Ancient Town: 30,000-50,000 VND ($1-2)
  • To An Bang Beach: 70,000-100,000 VND ($3-4)
  • To Da Nang Airport: 350,000-450,000 VND ($14-18)

Pros:

  • Fixed prices - no haggling
  • Track your ride in real-time
  • Driver ratings ensure good service
  • Cashless payment option

Traditional Taxis

Reputable companies: Mai Linh (green), Vinasun (white/green)

Typical Prices (metered):

  • Base fare: 10,000-15,000 VND
  • Per km: 12,000-15,000 VND
  • Within town: 40,000-80,000 VND ($1.50-3)

Taxi Tips:

  • Insist meter is turned on
  • Have destination address in Vietnamese
  • Carry small bills - some drivers claim no change
  • Screenshot your route on Google Maps
  • Avoid taxis at tourist hotspots (inflated prices)

Rent a bicycle, ride north into Cam Thanh village at sunrise. The early morning light on rice paddies is magical. Stop at a local cafe for Vietnamese coffee (15,000 VND) before returning.

Duration: Full day
Transport: Bicycle or scooter

Culture Day: My Son Temples

Distance: 80 km return
Duration: Full day
Transport: Scooter only

Leave early (6:30 AM), arrive at My Son by 7:30 AM before crowds and heat.
